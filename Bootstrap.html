FAQ

A: Parokya ni Edgar is a Filipino rock band formed in 1993, known for its original rock novelty songs and satirical covers of popular songs, both foreign and local. They are adept at playing various musical genres, including alternative rock, pop rock, funk, and rapcore.

A: The band consists of Chito Miranda (lead vocals), Gab Chee Kee (rhythm guitar, vocals), Buwi Meneses (bass guitar), Darius Semaña (lead guitar), Dindin Moreno (drums, percussion), and Vinci Montaner (backing vocals).

A: The name originated from a class joke during high school. When asked where Crisostomo Ibarra (a character from Jose Rizal’s novel Noli Me Tangere) was educated, a classmate jokingly answered "sa Parokya ni Edgar" ("at Edgar's Parish"). The band adopted this name for their first live performance, and it stuck. None of the band members are actually named Edgar.

A: Parokya ni Edgar has been called "The National Band of the Philippines" by local media. Their first album Khangkhungkherrnitz (1996) went triple platinum, selling 120,000 units. They have multiple platinum albums and won the Best Southeast Asian Music Video Award at the MTV Music Video Awards in 1999 for "Harana".

A: The band is known for their humorous and novelty rock songs but also covers a wide range of musical styles from rock to pop, funk, and rapcore, often providing comic relief in their music.

A: Parokya ni Edgar started as a group of college students who initially called themselves "Comic Relief." They gained exposure after opening for the Eraserheads and later signed with Universal Records. They began performing regularly at Club Dredd in Manila during the early 1990s.

A: The band was formed in 1993 and has been active for over 30 years, maintaining a significant presence in the Filipino music scene.

A: Some of their well-known studio albums include Khangkhungkherrnitz (1996), Buruguduystunstugudunstuy (1997), Gulong Itlog Gulong (1999), Bigotilyo (2003), Halina Sa Parokya (2005), and Middle-Aged Juvenile Novelty Pop Rockers (2010). They also have Christmas albums, compilation albums, and live albums.

A: Popular songs by Parokya ni Edgar include "Buloy," "Harana," "Picha Pie," "Inuman Na," "Mr. Suave," and "The Yes Yes Show." They are also known for their parody covers and humorous takes on popular songs.

Awards & Achievements

Dangal ng Musikang Filipino Award at the 37th Awit Awards (2024), recognizing their enduring influence and contribution to Filipino music over 31 years
Awit Awards multiple wins including:

• Best Novelty Recording for “Don’t Touch My Birdie” (1999)

• Best TV Theme Song for “!Oka Tokat” (1999)

• Best Alternative Recording for “Inuman Na” (2000)

• Best Album Package for Edgar Edgar Musikahan (2003)

• Multiple awards for “Mr. Suave” in 2004 including Record of the Year, Best Performance by a Duo or Group, Music Video Performance, and Best Novelty Recording

• Best Rap Recording for “The Yes Yes Show” (2004)

• Best Ballad Recording, Best Collaboration, Best Performance by a Group, and Best Song of the Year for “Paki-usap Lang” and “Pangarap Lang Kita” (2011).

NU Rock Awards wins including:

• Best New Artist and Best Live Act (1996)

• Song of the Year for “Don’t Touch My Birdie” (1998)

• Artist of the Year (1999, 2000, 2003)

• Best Album Packaging for Buruguduystunstugudunstuy (1998) and Edgar Edgar Musikahan (2002)

• Bassist of the Year for Buwi Meneses (1999)

MTV Asia Awards: Favorite Artist Philippines (2004)
